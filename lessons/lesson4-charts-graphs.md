# Lesson 4: Charts and Graphs 📊

**⏱️ Time:** About 25 minutes  
**What you'll learn:** How to turn your data into colorful, eye-catching charts!

---

## Why Use Charts?

Numbers in a table are useful, but a **chart** makes data easier to understand at a glance. Charts help you:

- Compare things (who scored the most?)
- See trends (are sales going up or down?)
- Show proportions (what percentage of votes did each candy get?)

---

## Step 1 — The Main Chart Types

| Chart Type | Best For | Example |
|------------|----------|---------|
| **Bar Chart** | Comparing items | Favorite subjects in class |
| **Column Chart** | Comparing items over time | Monthly rainfall |
| **Line Chart** | Showing trends | Temperature change over a week |
| **Pie Chart** | Showing percentages/parts | Percentage of votes |
| **Scatter Chart** | Showing relationships | Study time vs. grade |

---

## Step 2 — Creating Your First Bar Chart

Let's make a bar chart of game scores!

### Set Up the Data

Type this into a new spreadsheet:

| | A | B |
|-|---|---|
| **1** | Player | Score |
| **2** | Alex | 150 |
| **3** | Blake | 230 |
| **4** | Casey | 175 |
| **5** | Dana | 310 |
| **6** | Evan | 190 |

### Create the Chart

1. **Select the data:** Click **A1**, hold **Shift**, click **B6**.
   - You've selected **A1:B6**.
2. Click the **Insert** tab in the Ribbon.
3. In the **Charts** group, click the **Bar Chart** icon (it looks like sideways bars).
4. Choose **"Clustered Bar"** (the first option).
5. A chart appears!

---

## Step 3 — Moving and Resizing Your Chart

Your chart might be in the way. Let's move it!

**To move:** Click on the chart and **drag** it to a new location.

**To resize:** Click the chart, then drag the **corner handles** (small dots at the corners) to make it bigger or smaller.

### Try It!
Move your chart below your data table.

---

## Step 4 — Customizing Your Chart

Make your chart look awesome! When you click on a chart, new **Chart Tools** tabs appear at the top.

### Adding a Title
1. Click on the **chart title** text (it might say "Score" or "Chart Title").
2. **Double-click** to edit it.
3. Type **"Game Scores Comparison"**.
4. Press **Escape** when done.

### Changing Colors
1. Click on the chart to select it.
2. Click the **paintbrush icon** (🎨) that appears on the right side of the chart.
3. Click **"Color"** and choose a color scheme you like!

### Changing the Chart Style
1. Click the **paintbrush icon** (🎨).
2. Click **"Style"** and pick a style you like.

---

## Step 5 — Adding Data Labels

**Data labels** show the exact number on each bar, so readers don't have to guess.

1. Click on a **bar** in your chart to select all bars.
2. Right-click and choose **"Add Data Labels"**.
3. Now each bar shows its value!

---

## Step 6 — Creating a Pie Chart

Pie charts are great for showing how parts make up a whole.

### Set Up the Data

In a new area of your spreadsheet, type:

| | A | B |
|-|---|---|
| **1** | Candy Type | Votes |
| **2** | Chocolate | 45 |
| **3** | Gummy Bears | 30 |
| **4** | Lollipop | 15 |
| **5** | Sour Worms | 25 |
| **6** | Candy Corn | 10 |

### Create the Pie Chart
1. Select **A1:B6**.
2. Click **Insert** → **Pie Chart** icon.
3. Choose **"2-D Pie"** (the first option).
4. A pie chart appears! Each slice represents one candy type.

### Add Percentage Labels
1. Click on the pie chart.
2. Click the **+** (chart elements) button on the right.
3. Check **"Data Labels"**.
4. Right-click a data label, select **"Format Data Labels"**.
5. Check **"Percentage"** and uncheck **"Value"**.
6. Now your pie chart shows percentages! 🥧

## Step 7 — Creating a Line Chart

Line charts show how things change over time.

### Set Up the Data

| | A | B |
|-|---|---|
| **1** | Month | Temperature (°F) |
| **2** | January | 32 |
| **3** | February | 36 |
| **4** | March | 48 |
| **5** | April | 60 |
| **6** | May | 72 |
| **7** | June | 85 |

### Create the Line Chart
1. Select **A1:B7**.
2. Click **Insert** → **Line Chart** icon.
3. Choose **"Line with Markers"**.
4. Add a title: **"Monthly Temperatures"**
