# Lesson 2: Entering and Formatting Data ✏️

**⏱️ Time:** About 25 minutes  
**📚 What you'll learn:** How to type data into cells, edit it, and make it look great

---

## Step 1 — Typing Data Into Cells

Entering data in Excel is simple!

1. **Click on a cell** to select it.
2. **Start typing** — the text appears in the cell and the Formula Bar.
3. Press **Enter** to confirm and move down, or **Tab** to confirm and move right.

### Types of Data:
| Type | Example | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Text** | `Hello`, `Pokemon` | Left-aligned by default |
| **Numbers** | `42`, `3.14` | Right-aligned by default |
| **Dates** | `3/10/2025` | Excel recognizes most date formats |

### 🎯 Try It!
Open a new spreadsheet and type this data starting at **A1**:

| | A | B |
|-|---|---|
| **1** | Name | Score |
| **2** | Mario | 95 |
| **3** | Luigi | 87 |
| **4** | Peach | 92 |
| **5** | Toad | 78 |

## Step 2 — Editing and Deleting Data

**To edit a cell:**
- Double-click the cell to edit it directly, OR
- Click the cell and press **F2** to enter edit mode, OR
- Click the cell and retype — it replaces the old data

**To delete a cell's content:**
- Click the cell and press the **Delete** key

**To undo a mistake:**
- Press **Ctrl + Z** (Windows) or **Cmd + Z** (Mac)

### 🎯 Try It!
1. Click on cell **B3** (Luigi's score of 87).
2. Double-click it and change it to **90**.
3. Press **Enter**.
4. Press **Ctrl + Z** to undo — it goes back to 87!

---

## Step 3 — Selecting Multiple Cells

Sometimes you want to work with a group of cells at once.

| How to Select | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| A single cell | Click it |
| A range of cells | Click the first cell, hold **Shift**, click the last cell |
| A whole row | Click the **row number** on the left |
| A whole column | Click the **column letter** at the top |
| All cells | Press **Ctrl + A** |

A selected range is written like **A1:B5** (from A1 to B5).

### 🎯 Try It!
1. Click on **A1**.
2. Hold **Shift** and click on **B5**.
3. You've selected the range **A1:B5** — all your data!

## Step 4 — Making Text Look Great (Formatting!)

Formatting makes your spreadsheet easier to read and more fun! Find these buttons in the **Home** tab:

### 🔠 Font Formatting
| Button | What It Does | Shortcut |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **B** | **Bold** | Ctrl + B |
| *I* | *Italic* | Ctrl + I |
| <u>U</u> | Underline | Ctrl + U |
| Font Size | Make text bigger/smaller | — |
| Font Color | Change text color | — |

### 🎨 Cell Background Color
1. Select the cells you want to color.
2. Click the **Fill Color** button (the paint bucket 🪣 in the Home tab).
3. Pick your favorite color!

### 🎯 Try It!
1. Click cell **A1** ("Name") and hold Shift, click **B1** ("Score").
2. Press **Ctrl + B** to make the headers **bold**.
3. With A1:B1 still selected, click the **Fill Color** button and choose a light blue or yellow.
4. Your headers now stand out!

## Step 5 — Adjusting Column Width and Row Height

Sometimes your text is too wide to fit in a cell — you'll see `######` instead of the number, or text gets cut off.

**To fix column width:**
- **Auto-fit:** Double-click the border between two column letters (e.g., between **A** and **B**) — Excel sizes it perfectly!
- **Manual:** Click and drag the border to make it wider or narrower.

**To fix row height:**
- Double-click the border between two row numbers.

### 🎯 Try It!
1. Move your mouse to the line between column **A** and column **B** at the very top (between the letters).
2. When the cursor becomes a double arrow (↔), **double-click**.
3. Column A will auto-fit to the widest content!

---

## Step 6 — Aligning Text

You can align text left, center, or right in a cell.

1. Select the cells you want to align.
2. In the **Home** tab, find the alignment buttons:
   - ⬅️ Left Align
   - ⬛ Center
   - ➡️ Right Align

### 🎯 Try It!
1. Select **A1:B1** (your headers).
2. Click the **Center** button.
3. Your headers are now centered!

---

## Step 7 — Formatting Numbers

You can make numbers look better with special formats.

1. Select the number cells (**B2:B5**).
2. In the **Home** tab, find the **Number Format** dropdown (it might say "General").
3. Try these options:
   - **Number** — adds decimal places
   - **Currency** — adds a dollar sign 💰
   - **Percentage** — shows as %
