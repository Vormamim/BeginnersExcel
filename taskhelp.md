# Year 7 Digital Technology  
## Step-by-Step Guide: Collecting Data About Technology Use

### Big Question
**How is digital technology used in my household, and what impact does it have?**

You will collect real data from:
- Yourself
- ONE other person in your household

You will collect data for **3–5 days**.

---

# Step 1 — Understand the Two Types of Data

## Quantitative Data
Quantitative data is **numerical data**. It can be counted or measured.

Examples:
- 2 hours of screen time
- 3 devices used
- 4 times checking a phone
- 5 notifications during homework

These numbers can easily be turned into **graphs**.

---

## Qualitative Data
Qualitative data is **descriptive information**. It describes categories, opinions, or reasons.

Examples:
- Device used most (phone, laptop, console)
- Main purpose (gaming, school, social media)
- When technology helps
- When technology becomes distracting

Qualitative data helps explain **why something happens**.

---

# Step 2 — Choose Your Two Participants

You can only collect data from:

- **Person A:** Yourself  
- **Person B:** One household member

Possible choices:
- parent
- sibling
- grandparent
- carer

Do **not** include real names in your project.

---

# Step 3 — Ask Permission

Before collecting data, explain what you are doing.

Example:

> I am doing a school project about how technology is used in my household.  
> I will collect simple information like screen time and device type.  
> I will not collect private information or use names.  
> Is it okay if I include your answers?

---

# Step 4 — Protect Privacy

You must **never collect**:

- names
- passwords
- private messages
- personal accounts
- photos or screenshots

Your project must protect people’s privacy.

---

# Step 5 — Choose 4–6 Types of Data

You must collect **4–6 types of data**.

## Good Quantitative Options

- Screen time category (Low / Medium / High)
- Number of times technology is used
- Number of devices used
- Number of notifications during study

---

## Good Qualitative Options

- Device used most
- Main purpose of use
- When technology helps
- When technology causes distraction
- Household technology rules

---

# Step 6 — Choose an Investigation Scenario

## Scenario A: Screen Time and Distraction

Focus question:

**Does more screen time lead to more distraction?**

Collect:
- screen time category
- time of day used most
- when technology causes distraction
- main purpose of use

---

## Scenario B: Devices We Use

Focus question:

**Which devices are used most in my household?**

Collect:
- device type
- main purpose
- time of day used most
- when technology is helpful

---

## Scenario C: Helpful vs Distracting Technology

Focus question:

**When does technology help and when does it distract?**

Collect:
- when technology is helpful
- when technology causes distraction
- device used
- time of day used most
- household rules

---

## Scenario D: Student vs Adult Technology Use

Focus question:

**How is technology used differently by students and adults?**

Collect:
- screen time category
- device used most
- purpose of use
- time of day used most
- rules around technology

---

# Step 7 — Record Your Data

Collect data for **3–5 days**.

Example table:

| Day | Person | Screen Time | Device | Purpose | Time Used Most | Helpful When | Distracting When |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| Day 1 | A | Medium | Laptop | School | Afternoon | Homework | Notifications |
| Day 1 | B | High | Phone | Work | Morning | Email | During dinner |
| Day 2 | A | High | Console | Gaming | Evening | Relaxing | Bedtime |

---

# Step 8 — Organise Your Data

After collecting your data:

1. Sort the information into categories
2. Count how often each category appears
3. Prepare your results for graphs

Example categories:

Screen time:
- Low
- Medium
- High

Purpose:
- School
- Work
- Gaming
- Social
- Streaming

---

# Step 9 — Create Two Graphs

You must include **at least two graphs**.

Good examples:

### Graph 1
**Main Purpose of Technology Use**

A bar graph comparing:
- school
- gaming
- social
- streaming

---

### Graph 2
**Devices Used Most Often**

A pie chart or column graph showing:
- phone
- laptop
- console
- tablet

---

# Step 10 — Look for Patterns

Ask questions such as:

- Who uses technology more?
- Are the same devices used?
- When is technology most helpful?
- When is it most distracting?
- Are there rules that make a difference?

---

# Step 11 — Write an Ethics Statement

Example:

> I collected data from myself and one person in my household.  
> I asked permission before collecting the information.  
> I did not use names or collect private data.  
> The information was only used for my school project.

---

# Step 12 — Prepare Your Presentation

Your presentation should include:

1. Research question
2. How data was collected
3. Ethics and privacy
4. Graphs
5. One interesting finding
6. Your conclusion

Keep your presentation **clear and simple**.
